Typography
Type System

Breakers Slab Bold + Gin are the primary logo fonts, available via Adobe Fonts (CC subscription). Hatfield Park Stamp is the festival script — used sparingly, all instances outlined. Knockout covers supporting display needs. All print type will be outlined in final production files.

Licensing

All fonts requiring a license have been outlined in the working files — no additional software is needed to open or print production files. Breakers Slab and Gin are available via Adobe Fonts (fonts.adobe.com, CC subscription) for any new production work.

Notes on Refinements

After working through the iterations, the strongest direction is to stay within True North's established color system — added a subtle bronzy-gold in one iteration for depth. Pushed on a few with a blue background; also dabbled in using dark green as a highlight, but none of those versions outperform the original palette. There's also a practical upside: keeping last year's colors means the website and existing signage don't need to be rebuilt for year two. Tested a stacked horizontal layout with food above and music below — the volume of restaurant names creates too many lines of centered type, which adds visual noise and loses hierarchy at the margins.

The Brief
Year two. Bolder. More intentional.

True North is heading into 2026 with a bigger lineup, a stronger food story, and a cleaner design vision. Big Sis came in to elevate the poster and set the visual tone for the full campaign.

Creative Goal
Art Direction Refresh
Updated logo treatment, refined color palette, improved texture, and an overall aesthetic that reflects where the festival is headed — not just where it's been.
Content Challenge
Balance Without Flattening
Year two leans harder into food. The poster needs to honor that shift without subordinating music. Langhorne Slim headlines. Every element earns its place.
Deliverables
