The Brief

Year two. Bolder.
More intentional.

True North is heading into 2026 with a bigger lineup, a stronger food story, and a cleaner design vision. Big Sis came in to elevate the poster and set the visual tone for the full campaign.

Creative Goal
Art Direction Refresh
Updated logo treatment, refined color palette, improved texture, and an overall aesthetic that reflects where the festival is headed — not just where it's been.
Content Challenge
Balance Without Flattening
Year two leans harder into food. The poster needs to honor that shift without subordinating music. Langhorne Slim headlines. Every element earns its place.

Concept 1
01

Evolved palette. Elevated hierarchy.

Builds on the True North mark system while pushing the color story in a new direction. Food and music carry equal weight. The flag pole gets an update — the fork guitar appears as a way to visually bridge both sides of the festival.

Headliner
Langhorne Slim pulled out to a clear headliner position — unmissable at a glance, without crowding the food roster beneath.
Content Balance
Food and music given equal visual weight. Neither side of the festival is treated as supporting cast.
Visual Hierarchy
Clear read at distance — event name, headliner, date/venue, food + music — in that order, no competition.
Flag Pole Evolution
Testing a fork-guitar on the flag pole to symbolize both sides of the fest — food and music literally joined at the mark.
Alternate Colorways — Concept 1
Concept 2
02

True North at the center.

Explores whether the mark stands on its own — no flag pole update, no fork guitar. The logo anchors both sides of the festival, bridging food and music through placement and hierarchy rather than illustration. Pulls gold from year one; richer, deeper tones signal year two's evolution.

Mark Question
Does the True North logo need a flag pole update every year — or is it strong enough to stand on its own? This concept explores the latter.
Content Balance
Food and music balanced differently — True North sits at the center and bridges both, rather than dividing them into two columns.
Color Story
Pulls gold from year one as a thread of continuity, but surrounds it with richer, deeper tones that reflect where the festival is going.
